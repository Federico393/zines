Tarun Nayar
Modern Biology
How Tarun Nayar bridges nature and technology.

Tarun, you’re a former biologist, but what drew your inspiration to combine biology and sound for the creation of plant music?

Tarun

"I mean, honestly, it was not intentional. It was the culmination of just playing music and thinking about nature. I spent most of the pandemic up in the northern Gulf Islands, I have a little cabin out there, and I was just with all my synthesizers out in nature."

The video that went viral was the one where you create music with synthesizers and mushrooms. Mushrooms are known to be healing and spiritual. Would you say there’s a sense of spirituality in what you do?

Tarun

"Yeah, I like to think of what I do as some sort of a meeting place between biology, sound, and spirituality. My background is Indian, and the science of vibration is something very profound in the East."

You started touring as Modern Biology this year. Usually, you create these sounds in the comfort of your own space… I’m curious to know how the experience of playing with plants in front of others has been for you?

Tarun

"That’s a great question. Yeah, this is, like, my third show. I think I’m still figuring it out. Turning a 15-second video into a 45-minute set was definitely a challenge, but it’s exciting to dive deeper and make it more experimental."

Could you walk me through the technical process of creating music with bioelectricity and synthesizers?

Tarun

"The way I do it is based on lie detector technology. You send a small current through an organic body like a plant. As bioelectric changes occur, the current translates into note changes in synthesizers."
